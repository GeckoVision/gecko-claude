## Oracle, not bot
A **bot** executes. An **oracle** judges. Gecko is the oracle: it produces a *verdict* on whether an action is safe and sound, and any bot — ours or yours — acts on it. The execution venue (OKX, Jupiter, Kamino, Drift) is swappable plumbing.

## Verdict & dissent
A verdict is the panel's judgment plus the **surviving dissent** — the strongest argument *against* the action that wasn't refuted. A verdict with no honest dissent is a red flag, not a green light. Backtest verdicts are graded `DEPLOY / PAPER ONLY / REJECT`, and a clean **null** (no edge) is a valid result.

## Non-custodial custody
Signing happens inside the **OKX Agentic Wallet TEE** — the private key is generated in and never leaves the enclave. Alternatively, a **scoped embedded wallet** (per-tx cap, allowed venues) can be provisioned for one-click onboarding. Either way, Gecko never holds your raw key.

## Hurdle rate
A yield position must beat a **hurdle** to be worth the risk. Default is a Brazilian CDB-after-tax (~12%); a crypto-only hurdle is ~5.5%. Below the hurdle, the monitor rotates or exits.

## Liquidation buffer
For any leveraged position, the buffer is `1 − operating_LTV / liquidation_LTV` — how far collateral can fall before liquidation. The monitor compares the buffer to a **predicted downside** (derived from the same market-temperature read that gates trades) and de-leverages or exits before the buffer is breached.
