## The Receipt

The thing Gecko hands back. A **Receipt** is a verdict on a proposed move plus
everything behind it: the call, the opinions that disagreed, and the sources.
It's signed and shareable — an audit artifact you can put in front of a board or
a community to show the decision was checked.

## The verdict: act / pass / defer

Every Receipt carries one of three calls:

- **act** — the move is sound; proceed.
- **pass** — don't do it; here's the reason.
- **defer** — not now; wait for the condition named in the Receipt.

A `pass` or a `defer` is the product working, not failing.

## Surviving dissent

Gecko convenes a panel of independent expert voices, each arguing the move from
a different angle. The strongest argument *against* the move — the one that
didn't get refuted — **stays in the answer**, with its source. A verdict with no
honest dissent is a red flag, not a green light.

## Independence

Gecko has no position in the trade. It decides nothing, holds nothing, and never
takes a cut. That's why it can issue a check an agent can't issue about itself —
the same reason a company can't audit its own books.

## Non-custodial

Gecko never holds your money or your keys. Signing stays inside a secure enclave
or your own scoped wallet (with a per-trade cap and an allowed-venue list). The
off-switch can halt trading — but it can **never** block a withdrawal to your
own wallet.

## The proof agents

The trading agent and the profit vault you'll see in these docs are **proof
artifacts** — they exist to run real decisions through the verification layer so
you can watch it work (on live prices, in paper-trading mode). The product is the
**layer**, not the agents.
